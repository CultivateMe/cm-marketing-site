Engagement
Model

While every engagement is tailored to the unique needs of our clients, our work generally unfolds in the following stages:

  1. Explore

    Aims to… understand the current state and design a prototype solution

    Typically involves… 1:1 interviews, role design workshops, audit knowledge management systems, self-assessment

    You get… situational analysis, role sequences aligned to The Workforce Genome, solution prototype document

    Usually takes… 2-4 weeks

  2. Prototype

    Aims to… test the prototype solution at a small scale and tweak it

    Typically involves… simple learning system roll-out around a single topic (e.g. 1 product, 1 role, 1 sales skill, etc) with limited team, retrospectives and surveys

    You get… experiment report, revised learning system design document, engagement metrics tracker

    Usually takes… 1-2 months

  3. Refine

    Aims to… roll-out the revised learning system at full scale, usually in iterations

    Typically involves… integrating knowledge management systems, learning resource creation, team meetings and coaching, surveys and retrospectives

    You get… roll-out reports, engagement feedback, learning statistics

    Usually takes… 2-6 months

  4. Calibrate

    Aims to… embed the tools and practices and solidify the culture

    Typically involves… weekly team check-ins, triage meetings, minor system adjustments

    You get… weekly engagement reports and learning statistics

    Usually takes… 2-4 months

How We Work

  • Weekly Sprints: We define the work on Monday and we ship on Friday, week in and week out. All our work is tracked in Trello and completely transparent to you. You know exactly what’s happening at any time.
  • Levels of Engagement: Within our cadence of weekly sprints, you can tweak the level of engagement from 20% to 100% depending on your need. 20% engagements still ship every Friday, they just don’t ship as much stuff as a 100% engagement.
  • Prototype and Iterate: We believe in small experiments and frequent course correction. Each sprint is based on the last and we are constantly learning.
  • Gradual Release Of Responsibility: We design self-sustaining systems, i.e. we work ourselves out of a job. As the learning system and culture take hold we gradually remove ourselves and build capacity with internal champions.
